Frequently asked questions

  • What is Fit for Good (FFG) ?

    FFG is a programme specifically designed to help seniors enjoy becoming more mobile, more stable and more socially active in their community. We work with existing leisure and fitness organisations to provide a welcoming environment and help participants overcome obstacles to improving fitness.

  • How is this different from just going to a gym or exercise class?

    FFG is aimed at seniors who are doing little or no exercise and want to improve their health. We will be marketing the programme via GPs, local social and church groups and through influencers – the children or friends of people who could benefit. Our aim is to overcome negative perceptions about exercise and fitness facilities and to assist with clothing, transport and other issues that would hinder participation.

    FFG also offers social activity, through coffee/tea breaks following classes, a monthly event and a newsletter.

    Participants will be provided with a guide, to use while exercising at home.

  • How often should I come to a class?

    For maximum benefit, participants should come to at least two sessions a week. They will also be given exercises that can be done at home in between sessions.

  • How much does it cost?

    Each 90 minute session costs £7 (£5.60 plus VAT) although a variety of packages are available:
    - Buy 11 sessions for £77 and get one free (12 weeks)
    - Buy 23 sessions for £161 and get three free (26 weeks/six months)
    - Annual pass (a wonderful birthday, anniversary or holiday present!) -as many sessions as you wish for £500
    - Couples (married or living together) deal – buy any package and the second is 1/3 off

  • How do I buy a package?

    To keep costs as low as possible, payments must be done by bank transfer or standing order. In special circumstances we can accept cheques although this will incur a small additional charge to cover the bank charge. Trainers will not be able to accept cash nor can we offer ‘pay as you attend’.

  • What insurance do you have?

    All FFG trainers have their own insurance and the company carries additional coverage.

  • Why should I join/Why should my parent or relative join?

    FFG training can help increase mobility, stability, bone density and general fitness, allowing participants to have a more active, healthier life.FFG also offers opportunities for companionship and community service, keeping seniors engaged with their communities.

  • What credentials do you have?

    All trainers will be qualified personal trainer practitioners. Some are also qualified to accept GP referrals.

  • What should I expect in an FFG class?

    Following your induction, you will join at least two, one-hour class sessions a week. These will offer a range of activities geared to your ability. You may be challenged to do more than you thought you could but you will never be pushed to exhaustion. After the classes, free coffee and tea will be available, and you will be have a chance to get to know others attending.

  • What happens during the induction?

    During your induction, you will fill out a short form which will help your FFG trainer to understand your fitness goals and will help you to set realistic short-term and long-term goals. If you have existing health issues, these must be mentioned. A letter from your GP allowing you to participate may be needed.

  • How do I sign up and pay for classes?

    Prospective participants will be able to join via the FFG website, by calling the FFG office, or by signing up during special sessions in local village halls and GP surgeries.

    To keep costs to a minimum, payment is by bank transfer, with an 11 session minimum membership.

  • What if I become ill or can’t continue?

    You’ll be able put your membership on hold until you are well or, with a GP note, you’ll be refunded your unused fee.

  • What should I wear?

    Comfortable clothing and trainers or plimsolls. FFG has an expert clothing consultant who can help you order appropriate clothing if you wish.

  • Do I need any special equipment?

    All equipment needed will be provided in the class. If you wish to purchase equipment for home use, we can help you buy what you need.

  • How do I get there?

    If you don’t drive and can’t walk to the FFG session venue, we can help arrange community transport or a car pool.
