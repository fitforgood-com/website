About Jordan

What got you into fitness?

As a teenager I was overweight, but I got tired of feeling lethargic all the time, so I joined a gym at 15 and the rest is history. I was amazed at how good exercising made me feel. It gave me the drive to work hard at school and greatly improved my mood. I started to enjoy life a bit more and my confidence improved. Ten years later I decided that personal training was my passion - after I took my mum through the same journey that I went on. She lost three stone and now runs for a running club three times a week.

What inspires you?

My main inspiration is seeing what my body is capable of. There is no limit on what the human body can do and I am always finding new and challenging ways to move and improve my fitness. Seeing others realise their potential also motivates me - if I had a penny for every time a client has told me “I cannot do that” and then goes on to exceed their expectations then I’d be rich!

Free time activities?

In my spare time I like to completely relax - whether that’s just chilling on the sofa or a weekend away. I spend my life on my feet and teaching fitness, so time away is crucial. It allows me to be the best I can be when I go into work every day.

Any pets?

No pets but wherever I’ve lived, cats have always come to visit. It’s like they know I’m there! I currently live in a first floor flat and a cat called Dora managed to get in through the window. I have no idea how she managed to climb up.

Top training tip?

Remember to breathe whilst exercising. It sounds silly but a lot of people hold their breath when partaking in physical activity - due to nervousness or the adrenaline of moving in a challenging way. Long and deep inhales and exhales, breathing through the nose and out of the mouth, will help you relax and lower your heart rate, which in turn will allow you to continue for longer without having to stop to take a break.

About Rick

What got you into fitness?

I came into fitness later in life at age 30 in 1999 after being diagnosed with Diabetes, high Cholesterol, high blood pressure and being told I had a 80% risk of a heart attack all following a life time of bad eating habits. I made a commitment to myself there and then that I would loose 6 stone (38kg) of body fat and exercise myself back to fitness (which I did!) and then help others to do the same too. 

What inspires you?

I’m inspired everyday by someone or something, today it happens to be a middle aged man who comes regularly to my class and never misses a week. Why is this so inspirational? He has learning and communication difficulties, however throughout the class he moves and flows perfectly along with everyone else and to an onlooker he would just appear as everyone else does. He has gained so much from the classes, fat loss, muscle strength, community, and most importantly acceptance from the group.

Free time activities?

Honestly I don’t have much free time as I spend so much time working in the fitness industry and further studying for fitness related exams to broaden my knowledge. When I do get time I enjoy walking the dog with my husband who also works in fitness. I also enjoy a variety of music and for fun watching ‘conspiracy theory’ videos on YouTube. 

Any pets ?

Yes a dog. A very large Dogue de Boudeaux who weighs around 10 stone (65kg). He is an amazing dog with a beautiful temperament.

Top training tip?

Don’t give up! Find something that works for you so you can keep going, sometimes it may take a few different things or tries before you find what feels right. Fitness is genuinely life changing as it did change mine and it will yours too.

Fit for Good

Most seniors would like to improve their fitness, but obstacles like cost, clothing, transport and shyness defeat these aspirations. Fit for Good helps overcome these impediments and provides motivation to increase fitness in the UK’s fastest growing demographic, using existing local resources. Our aim is to bring a new demographic into fitness and ensuring exercise becomes a part of members’ daily lives

Elizabeth Hunt

Director, Fit for Good

About Elizabeth

What got you into fitness?

My family has always been fairly active. My grandfather loved hiking and skiing which he passed on to all of us. He took up running in his 60s and kept at it into his 90s. My grandmother was a great swimmer, in both the ocean and in pools. Every winter she’d set off for a couple of weeks to try a new swimming beach in some exotic location. My parents loved playing tennis and sailing. My sister is amazing – still climbing mountains and skiing in her 60s.

What inspires you?

I love the idea that we have some control over how we age - and that Fit for Good can help people enjoy a healthier and happier ageing process.

Free time activities?

Not a lot of free time but when we can, we are often gardening and dog walking - and we love garden visiting through the National Gardens Scheme.

Any pets?

A soft-coated wheaten terrier – one of the best dogs we’ve ever had, a very sweet girl.

Top training tip?

Don’t give up! Some days training is great, on others - not so good. But they all add up to a much healthier and happier life.
